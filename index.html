Emberfall, a playable arcade game where you defend a forest from falling embers and spreading fire. Tap embers and beetles to swat them. A toolkit on the left lets you select tools: Water douses burning trees and knocks down dead snags; Seed plants regular seedlings; Climate plants fire- and beetle-resistant seedlings from a stock that refills over time; Line digs a firebreak that blocks spread once built; Thin sends a chainsaw crew to cut a wider fuel break in overstocked patches; Rx Burn periodically sends a slow controlled flame across the forest floor to clear standing snags and beetle fuel when enough points and cooldown are available; Beetle sprays verbenone to repel bark beetles from an area; and Air calls a retardant tanker once you have enough points. Scorched trees stop producing cones, stand dead, then shed needles into snags that breed beetle outbreaks; snags can catch fire, and a second burn makes them fall. Woodpeckers arrive to eat beetles, dense forests spread fire faster, surviving trees reseed burned ground with cones, climate-adapted trees produce climate-adapted seedlings, rain becomes less frequent, and embers fall faster as time passes.
